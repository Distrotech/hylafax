K56flex MODEMS

October, 1997

INTRODUCTION

October 1997
There are rivals in the new 56K modem technology ~ US Robotics was the first company to introduce a modem with the x2 standard. Rockwell and Lucent came up with a chip set using a version called K56flex.

The ITU (International Telecommunications Union) has still to decide which will become the offical standard, with both a determining standard and a final standard to be settled.

NOTES
  • The Multitech K56flex modem ( rom level reports as 'V1.01-K56-DLS') runs okay in Class 1 but cannot cope with adaptive answer.
  • Similar problems to the earlier V34bis are reported for the new USR Sportster 56K/x2. Illegal characters where CR-LF pairs should be are causing Unspecified Transmit Phase B errors. The best suggestion at the moment is to run this modem in Class 1.
